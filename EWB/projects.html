Our international project is to partner with La Asociación de Caficultores de Las Lajas (ACOLL) in La Victoria, Ecuador, and construct a pipeline and irrigation system for agricultural improvements.



Needs of the Community

Las Lajas is a mid-size county located in the southern part of Ecuador in the province of El Oro. There are several communities that make up this county. Approximately 5,000 people live in Las Lajas (2,500 in La Victoria). Most are farmers, and they mainly produce coffee. The climate is hot and dry, and rainfall can be unreliable and insufficient.

The Association of Organic Coffee Producers of Las Lajas (ACOLL) and farmers would like to establish a water collection, storage, and distribution system to stabilize their water supply and optimize cultivation in every season. In this project, the USC chapter of EWB-USA will design and oversee construction of an system to improve irrigation and water quality for the community.

The community will provide machinery and labor for the construction process. Their main concern is about the long-term stability of the system, so establishing a sense of ownership and ensuring good education about maintenance and repair are key aspects of the project. The chapter is committed to the Las Lajas project for a minimum of five years (beginning in 2012).

Pre-Assessment Trip (Winter 2012)

This trip was our first introduction to La Victoria community, giving us a chance to build a relationship with them and also survey the local terrain. Based on this preliminary knowledge, we were able to proceed with designing an appropriate project.
